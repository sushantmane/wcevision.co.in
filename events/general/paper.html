19th and 20th Feb. 2014

PAPER

. . . . a paper presentation competition.

Introduction

With the development of new technology and industrialization, gasping new heights, presentation skills have gained overwhelming impact. So let us take your self confidence, technical knowledge get tested . Get ready for the awakening.

Details

Novice Group:Students of F.E., S.E., F.Y. & S.Y.
Expert Group:Students of T.E., B.E., M.E. & T.Y.

Rule and Regulations

  • 1. Participants will be provided with 3 statements of which they have to present a solution on any one for expert group and topics will be provided for novice group.
  • 2. Novice groups need to send an IEEE standard abstract based on their topic. The abstract not exceed 100 words and should contain topic name, objective of paper & outcome. Mention your name and contact number in abstract.
  • 3. Expert groups need to send a proper solution of statements. The solution should be in IEEE standard format including their names and contacts.
  • 4. Teams will be shortlisted according to abstract and solution.
  • 5. The video should be made by them depending upon their solution.
  • 6. Time allotment
    Paper presentation: 7 min.
    Conclusion: 1 min.
    Video: 2 min.
    Question-answer: 2 min.
  • 7. Video may be readymade or self-made.
  • 8. Decision made by judge will be final & mandatory to all.

Topics For Paper Presentation

Computer Science and Engineering / Information Technology

Expert:

1. Machine Learning

How can we apply machine learning in day-to-day life scenario? Demonstrate by solving Some problems related to current working environments. Many satellites, rockets, robots, AI systems support it.

2. Ubiquitous (Pervasive) Computing

Ubiquitous computing can occur using any device, in any location, and in any format. A user interacts with the computer, which can exist in many different forms, including laptop computers, tablets, terminals and phones. The underlying technologies to support ubiquitous computing include Internet, advanced middleware, operating system, mobile code, sensors, microprocessors, New I/O and user interfaces, networks, mobile protocols, location and positioning and new materials. e.g. Virtual Reality.

3. Disaster Management System

Disaster occurs in several forms. Ultimately any problem can be problem by using means of Technology. Devise a system that can manage accidents (Loss of Life) occurring in kumbh mela, India. Estimated 700 people lose their life due to congestion.
